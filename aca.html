Autism Camp Australia

Website redesign

Challenge

The Autism Camp Australia website overwhelms users with large sections of information and lacks obvious signifiers that direct users to the enrolment form. This severely limits users from enrolling in the camps

Solution

Redesign the homepage and camp enrolment flow so users can clearly locate the enrolment form by providing appropriate signifiers and better chunking information to reduce information overload.

My role

Conducted user tests and secondary research, developed the persona and user journey map, and developed the wireframes for the three camp programs pages.

Timing

Three weeks

Research

UX Research

I conducted five user tests to identify how quickly users could locate the enrolment form for the Victorian camp.

None of the users were able to locate the enrolment form within five-minutes, as it was unexpectedly hidden as a hyperlink under blue text (see image).

Research findings

Due to time and resource constraints, I examined existing surveys on caregivers of autistic young people. The most common problems experienced by caregviers are:

  • Interruption of personal time,
  • Disruption of family’s social activities,
  • Less attention to other family members.

    • Persona

    The research insights allowed me to take an empathetic approach to the persona development. Our persona, Jessica, worries her autistic son is socially isolated and struggles to find personal time among her caregiving responsibilities.

    How might we assure Jessica that our camps will provide her with temporary respite from her caregiving duties while her son feels safe, cared for and socially engaged?

    Ideation

    Journey map

    I mapped Jessica's journey from the discovery phase to participating in the camp. The contemplation phase is where the redesign can make the greartest impact, particilarly by addressing any hesitations.

    User flow

    The user flow simply illustrates the path a user would take to enrol in a camp. User would initially select a camp, and then click the enrolment button once they were ready to enrol. This was a much simpler improvement as it eliminated several steps for users.

    Competitor analysis

    A scan of similar local non-for-profit websites helped identify opportunities for improvement, namely the structure of the navigation bar.

    The navigation bar contained 11 links, which was a hurdle for our users in identifying the right category, particularly as the links would take up multiple lines on smaller desktop screens.

    Information architecture

    We brainstormed ways to reduce the navigation bar to a maximum of five links. There were four broad categories of actions available to users, including enrolment, donations, information and contact.

    It’s time to just be you.

    Building

    Sketching and wireframing

    I led the redesign of the camp pages. I initially created a low-fidelity wireframe that I shared with my team and friends for early feedback. I then proceeded with a mid-fidelity wireframe to better visualise the design and create a clickable prototype for testing.

    Responsive design

    A 12 column grid was applied to all screens. Breakpoints were applied at ‘max-width: 960px’ for tablet and ‘max-width: 550px’ for mobile.

    Testing

    I conducted several rounds of user tests on the mid-fidelity wireframes to identify any barriers user's experienced when trying to enrol.

    Users noted the large amounts of information. A sidebar was added to help users navigate to key information. Further iterations involved consolidating key information in a discrete section labelled "The Package".

    Style Guide

    The style guide mostly reflects the original colour pallette and type face choices. Slight tweaks were made to ensure the colours met W3C accessibility standards.

    Final design

    Homepage

    The homepage was redesigned to explain more about what the organisation does and why. Information is organised in discrete sections to make it easier for users to digest key information.

    Camp pages

    The NSW, Vic, and Qld camp pages were redesigned to provide improved hierarchy of information and easy navigation with the addition of a sidebar.
